Meme Community respects intellectual property rights and complies with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA). This policy outlines our procedures for handling copyright infringement claims.

Reporting Copyright Infringement

If you believe that content on our platform infringes your copyright, please send a DMCA takedown notice to our designated agent with the following information:

  • A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized agent
  • Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed
  • Identification of the infringing material and its location on our platform (URL)
  • Your contact information (name, address, phone, email)
  • A statement that you have a good faith belief the use is not authorized
  • A statement that the information is accurate and you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner

Counter-Notification

If your content was removed due to a DMCA notice and you believe it was removed in error, you may send a counter-notification with:

  • Your physical or electronic signature
  • Identification of the removed content and its location before removal
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that the content was removed by mistake
  • Your contact information
  • Consent to the jurisdiction of your local federal court

Fair Use & Memes

Memes are often considered transformative works protected under fair use doctrine. However, each case is evaluated individually. We consider factors such as:

  • The purpose and character of the use
  • The nature of the copyrighted work
  • The amount used relative to the original work
  • The effect on the market value of the original work

Repeat Infringers

Users who repeatedly infringe copyright may have their accounts terminated. We maintain records of DMCA notices for this purpose.
